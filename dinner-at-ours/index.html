Slow Cooker Butternut Squash & Red Lentil Curry

Low: 6–7 hrs High: 3–4 hrs

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed (~4 cups)
  • 1 cup dried red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp salt, black pepper to taste

Optional Add-ins

  • 1 cup diced carrots
  • Handful of kale or spinach
  • Pinch of cayenne

Instructions

  1. Add squash, lentils, onion, garlic, and ginger to the slow cooker.
  2. Sprinkle in curry powder, turmeric, cumin, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour in vegetable broth and coconut milk. Stir gently.
  4. Cover and cook on Low 6–7 hours or High 3–4 hours until lentils are soft and squash is tender.
  5. Stir well before serving. Adjust seasoning.
  6. If adding greens, stir in during the last 10–15 minutes.

Serving & Storage

Serve over basmati rice, quinoa, with naan, or on its own. Keeps 4–5 days refrigerated. Freezes beautifully. Thickens overnight—add a splash of broth when reheating.

Slow Cooker Potato Leek Soup

Serves 6 Prep: 15 min Low: 6–8 hrs High: 3–4 hrs Vegan

Ingredients

  • 3 large leeks (white and light-green parts), cleaned and sliced thin
  • 4 cups potatoes, diced (Yukon Gold or russets)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or vegan butter
  • 4 cups vegetable broth (plus up to 1 cup water)
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt & black pepper to taste
  • 1 cup unsweetened oat milk or cashew milk
  • Optional: 1 tsp white miso paste or liquid smoke

Instructions

  1. Prep leeks: Cut off roots and dark green tops. Slice lengthwise, rinse thoroughly between layers. Slice into half-moons.
  2. Optional sauté: Heat olive oil in skillet. Sauté leeks, carrots, celery, and garlic 6–8 min until softened and fragrant.
  3. Combine in slow cooker with potatoes, broth, bay leaf, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir.
  4. Cook on Low 6–8 hours or High 3–4 hours until potatoes are fork-tender.
  5. Blend: Remove bay leaf. Use immersion blender for smooth or blend half for chunky.
  6. Finish: Stir in oat/cashew milk for creaminess. Add miso paste for umami if desired. Adjust seasoning.
  7. Garnish with chives, parsley, drizzle of olive oil, or smoked paprika.

Variations

Use blended soaked cashews instead of plant milk for extra creaminess. Add ½ cup red lentils for body and protein. Add rosemary or tarragon. Stir in 1 tsp Dijon mustard. Toss in kale or spinach during the last 30 minutes.

Slow-Cooker Curried Butternut Squash & Apple Soup

Serves 4–6 Prep: 15 min Low: 6–7 hrs High: 3–4 hrs Vegan

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash (~6 cups), peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 2 medium apples, peeled, cored, and chopped (Honeycrisp, Cortland, or Empire)
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp olive oil or vegan butter
  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 (13.5 oz) can coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup (optional)
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Instructions

  1. Optional sauté: Warm oil in skillet. Sauté onion and garlic 3–4 min. Add curry powder and cumin, cook 1 min more.
  2. Load slow cooker: Transfer onion mixture (or add raw), squash, apples, broth, maple syrup, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Cook on Low 6–7 hours or High 3–4 hours until squash and apples are very tender.
  4. Blend: Add coconut milk. Purée until silky with immersion blender. Thin with extra broth if needed.
  5. Finish: Stir in lemon juice. Adjust salt and pepper.
  6. Garnish with toasted pumpkin seeds, swirl of coconut milk, and chopped cilantro or parsley.

Serving Suggestions

Serve with crusty bread or roasted Brussels sprouts. Pairs beautifully with a kale-apple salad with maple-Dijon vinaigrette. Excellent as a make-ahead first course for Thanksgiving-style dinners.

Slow Cooker Pinto Bean & Lentil Chili

High: 4 hrs Low: 6–8 hrs

Ingredients

  • 1 (15 oz) can pinto beans, drained
  • 1 (15 oz) can tomato sauce
  • 1 cup brown lentils, rinsed (or French green lentils)
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3–4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 bell pepper (any color), diced
  • 1 can corn, drained (or frozen corn—see notes)
  • ⅔ cup tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp cacao powder
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • Tiny pinch of cayenne pepper (for heat)
  • 1–2 tsp honey (use less—a little goes a long way)
  • 3 cups water

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients to slow cooker with 3 cups water. If using canned corn, add it now. If using frozen corn, see notes for timing.
  2. Stir to combine.
  3. Cook on High for 4 hours or Low for 6–8 hours.
  4. Adjust seasoning and serve.

Notes

Frozen corn: If using frozen corn instead of canned, add it during the last 45–90 min if cooking on Low, or the last 30–45 min if cooking on High.

Lentil swap: French green lentils work great here and hold their shape well.

Marinara shortcut: If using store-bought marinara, skip the tomato paste, tomato sauce, and honey. Use about ¾ of a marinara jar instead.

Slow Cooker Creamy Tomato Lentil Soup

Yields 4–6 servings High: 4 hrs

Ingredients

  • 2–3 large beefsteak tomatoes (or 3–4 if medium), roughly chopped, divided
  • 1½ cups French green lentils, rinsed
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed and chopped
  • 1 large basil sprig plus 1 cup roughly chopped basil
  • ¼ cup tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1½ tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Paprika, turmeric, and cumin (to taste)
  • Black pepper
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • ¾ cup coconut cream (1:1 substitute for heavy cream)
  • Green onions, for topping

Instructions

  1. In a 6–8 quart slow cooker, combine 5 cups water, about 1 pound of the tomatoes (with juices), lentils, garlic, basil sprig, tomato paste, olive oil, salt, sugar, oregano, paprika, turmeric, cumin, and generous grinds of black pepper. Cook on High for 4 hours.
  2. Remove and discard the basil sprig (it's OK if it falls apart). Whisk the soup vigorously to help the tomatoes and lentils break down and become smooth.
  3. Stir in the remaining tomatoes and chopped basil, plus the lemon juice and coconut cream. Taste for seasoning. The soup will thicken as it cools—add more water as needed. Top with sliced green onions to serve.

Notes

Coconut cream is a 1:1 substitute for heavy cream. Use 2–3 large beefsteak tomatoes or 3–4 medium. French green lentils hold their shape nicely in this soup.

Lentil & Vegetable Slow Cooker Stew

Serves 4–6 Low: 6–8 hrs / High: 3½–4½ hrs

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 ribs celery, sliced
  • 1 sweet potato or regular potato, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 cups spinach or kale (optional)
  • Lemon juice, for finishing (optional)

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients except greens and lemon to the slow cooker. Stir to combine.
  2. Cook on Low for 6–8 hours or High for 3½–4½ hours, until lentils and vegetables are tender.
  3. Remove the bay leaf. Stir in spinach or kale and let wilt for 5–10 minutes.
  4. Season with salt, pepper, and a squeeze of lemon juice to taste.

Notes

Reduce broth to 3½ cups for a thicker stew. Add a can of chickpeas for extra protein. Try adding coriander or curry powder for a spiced version. Freezer-friendly for up to 3 months.

Curried Lentils with Kale & Sweet Potato

Serves 4–6 Prep: 15 min Cook: 35–40 min Vegan · One-Pot

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil or coconut oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced (or 1 tsp ground)
  • 1½ tbsp curry powder
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes (~3 cups), peeled and diced ¾-inch
  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and diced (optional)
  • 3 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 (13.5 oz) can full-fat coconut milk
  • 3 cups kale, chopped (stems removed)
  • Salt & black pepper to taste
  • Juice of ½ lime or lemon
  • Optional toppings: cilantro, toasted coconut, pumpkin seeds

Instructions

  1. Sauté the aromatics: Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, cook 5–7 min until softened. Add garlic and ginger, cook 1 min.
  2. Bloom the spices: Stir in curry powder, cumin, and turmeric. Cook 30 seconds to toast.
  3. Add vegetables and lentils: Add sweet potatoes, carrot, and lentils. Stir to coat.
  4. Simmer: Add broth and coconut milk. Bring to gentle boil, reduce to low. Cover and simmer 25–30 min until tender and thickened.
  5. Add the kale: Stir in kale, cook uncovered 5–7 min until wilted.
  6. Finish: Stir in lime/lemon juice. Adjust seasoning.
  7. Serve with brown rice, quinoa, or warm naan.

Variations

Add a drained can of chickpeas for extra protein. Stir in 1 tbsp almond or peanut butter for richness. Add ¼ tsp cayenne for heat. Add diced bell pepper or cauliflower florets.

Easy Red Lentil Dhal

Serves 4 Nut Free Freezes Well

Ingredients

  • 1 cup split red lentils
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 1 green chile, seeded and finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2 tbsp medium curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1×14oz can coconut milk
  • 1 tsp coconut sugar
  • 2 large handfuls baby spinach (~3½ oz)
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Put lentils in large saucepan, cover with boiling water. Simmer 5 min until softened but still with bite. Drain. Be careful not to overcook.
  2. In large skillet over medium heat, add coconut oil, onion, garlic, chile, and a pinch of salt. Sauté until soft. Add all spices, stir through 1 min.
  3. Turn heat down. Pour in coconut milk and coconut sugar. Bring to boil, stirring to combine.
  4. Stir in drained lentils. Cook in the sauce 5 more min. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add spinach, stir until wilted. Add lemon juice and serve.

Notes

You can substitute all the spices (coriander, cumin, cinnamon, curry powder, turmeric) with ⅓ of a can of yellow curry paste.

Cauliflower Tikka Masala

Serves 4 45 min

Ingredients

  • 2 oz unsalted cashews
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 1 tbsp mango chutney
  • 1 lemon
  • 2 tbsp plain yogurt
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Olive oil
  • 7 oz paneer cheese
  • ½ head cauliflower (14 oz)
  • 1 knob unsalted butter
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 3 cloves
  • 3 cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 onion
  • 2-inch piece fresh ginger
  • 2 tbsp sun-dried tomato paste
  • 4 sprigs fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. Put cashews, saffron, and mango chutney in pitcher with 3 cups boiling water. Set aside to soak.
  2. Preheat broiler to high. Grate lemon zest into bowl, add yogurt, paprika, salt, pepper, and 1 tbsp oil. Mix well.
  3. Chop paneer into ¾-inch cubes, break cauliflower into florets. Toss in marinade. Broil on middle rack 12 min until golden.
  4. Melt butter with 1 tsp oil in casserole pan on low. Bash cardamom pods for inner seeds. Add cinnamon, cloves, and cardamom seeds. Finely slice garlic, cook a few min.
  5. Peel and chop onion and ginger. Blend with tomato paste and splash of boiling water into a paste. Pour into pan, cook 10 min stirring regularly.
  6. Blend cashew mixture until super-smooth. Pour into pan, bring to boil, simmer 5 min.
  7. Stir in paneer and cauliflower. Season to perfection. Place under broiler until golden and bubbling. Stir and pick over cilantro.

Notes

Serve with fluffy rice and lemon wedges. Swap paneer for chickpeas and cauliflower for squash for a different but equally delicious result.

Masala Cauliflower & Sweet Potatoes

Serves 3–4

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • ½ tsp mustard seeds
  • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 yellow onion, sliced
  • 1 (1-inch) knob fresh ginger, minced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 (15-oz) can crushed tomatoes (or 4 medium, diced)
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp ground cayenne pepper
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 head cauliflower, cut into florets
  • 1 sweet potato, sliced
  • 2 green chiles, slit open lengthwise
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Handful of fresh cilantro, chopped

Cilantro-Mint Chutney

  • 1 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • ½ cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1 green chile
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Kosher salt

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in skillet over medium heat. Add mustard and cumin seeds, sizzle a few seconds. Add sliced onions, cook 5–7 min until lightly browned.
  2. Add ginger and garlic, cook another 2 min.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook 3–4 min. Add cumin, cayenne, turmeric, and salt. Stir, cover, and let masala cook 3–4 min.
  4. Add cauliflower florets and sweet potato slices. Stir to coat. Cover and cook, stirring frequently, 15–20 min until tender. Season.
  5. Add green chiles, lemon juice, and cilantro.
  6. Chutney: Blend cilantro, mint, chile, cumin, and lemon juice in high-speed blender. Add a bit of water if needed. Season with salt. Serve alongside.

Satay Sweet Potato Curry

Serves 4 (with rice) Freezes Well

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 3 tbsp melted coconut oil
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp medium curry powder
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, diced
  • 1×14oz can coconut milk
  • 1 tsp coconut sugar
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • ⅓ cup smooth peanut butter
  • 1×14oz can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tsp tamari
  • 2 handfuls spinach (~3½ oz)
  • Large handful peanuts, toasted and chopped
  • Handful cilantro (~½ cup), chopped
  • Salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss sweet potatoes with salt, 2 tbsp coconut oil, chilli powder, cumin, and curry powder. Roast 20 min.
  2. In large heavy saucepan, heat remaining 1 tbsp coconut oil. Add onion and garlic, cook 5–10 min until soft.
  3. Add coconut milk and coconut sugar. Bring to boil, then simmer about 10 min.
  4. Add lime juice, peanut butter, chickpeas, and tamari. Simmer 5 min. Add roasted sweet potatoes and about ½ cup boiling water. Cook 5 more min.
  5. Stir through spinach and let stand until wilted. Serve in bowls with toasted peanuts and cilantro.

Notes

If sauce looks like it's splitting, add a splash of almond or oat milk. Great served with roast cauliflower (425°F, salt, pepper, paprika, 15–20 min).

Spinach & Chickpea Curry

Serves 4 (with rice) Nut Free Freezes Well

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 9 oz spinach
  • 1×14oz can coconut milk
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and finely chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tbsp medium curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 24 cherry tomatoes, sliced
  • 2×14oz cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • ½ lemon
  • 2 tbsp coconut yogurt
  • Pinch of chilli flakes (optional)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Cook coconut oil, onion, garlic, and celery with salt and pepper 5–10 min until softened.
  2. In separate pan, wilt half the spinach with splash of boiling water. Add half the coconut milk and blend with immersion blender until smooth.
  3. Add ginger, mustard seeds, coriander, curry powder, and cumin to onion mixture. Toast 1 min.
  4. Add puréed spinach, cherry tomatoes, chickpeas, and remaining coconut milk with generous salt and pepper.
  5. Bring to bubble, then simmer 15–20 min. Add remaining spinach for final few minutes. Finish with lemon squeeze, yogurt, and chilli flakes.

Notes

If freezing, set aside what you want to freeze before adding the yogurt.

Jackfruit Curry

Serves 4 (with rice) Nut Free Freezes Well

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp medium curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp chilli powder
  • ½ tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 2×14oz cans jackfruit, drained and rinsed
  • 2×14oz cans coconut milk
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • Salt and pepper

Coconut Rice (optional)

  • 4 portions brown rice (~1½ cups)
  • 1×14oz can coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened desiccated coconut

Instructions

  1. Coconut rice (if making): Combine rice, coconut milk, 1¼ cups water, and pinch of salt. Bring to boil, reduce, simmer 45–50 min. Stir through desiccated coconut.
  2. Heat coconut oil in medium saucepan. Cook onion and garlic 10 min until starting to soften.
  3. Add all spices, stir through, cook 1 more min.
  4. Add jackfruit and mix well. Add coconut milk, lime juice, and maple syrup.
  5. Cook 25–30 min, stirring every 5–10 min, until jackfruit is really soft.
  6. Using wooden spoon, break down any large chunks of jackfruit to shred it.
  7. Mix well and serve with coconut rice or plain rice.

Chickpea & Riced Cauliflower Curry

Serves 3–4

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 yellow onion, chopped
  • 1 (1-inch) knob fresh ginger, grated
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 (15.5-oz) can chickpeas, drained
  • 1 (15-oz) can crushed tomatoes with juice
  • 2 cups riced cauliflower
  • 1 (13.5-oz) can full-fat coconut milk
  • ½ lemon
  • Fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
  • Toasted bread (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in large saucepan over medium heat. Sauté onions 3–4 min until tender. Add ginger, cook 2–3 min. Season with salt and pepper.
  2. Stir in turmeric, cumin, and cayenne. Cook 2–3 min until masala infuses with onions.
  3. Add chickpeas. Cook 5–10 min until starting to brown slightly.
  4. Add tomatoes. Simmer about 10 min, stirring frequently.
  5. Add riced cauliflower. Cook about 3 min.
  6. Stir in coconut milk. Simmer 20–30 min until thickened to desired consistency.
  7. Season. Finish with squeeze of lemon and cilantro. Enjoy with toasted bread.

Notes

Use packaged or frozen riced cauliflower, or make your own by blitzing cauliflower florets in a food processor or high-speed blender.

Eggplant Lasagna with Noodles

Serves 6–8

Ingredients

Eggplant

  • 1–2 large eggplants, sliced lengthwise ¼-inch thick
  • Olive oil
  • Salt

Pasta

  • 9–12 lasagna noodles (regular or no-boil)

Cheese Mixture

  • 15 oz whole-milk ricotta
  • 1 large egg
  • 1½ cups shredded mozzarella, divided
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1–2 tbsp fresh basil or parsley, chopped
  • Black pepper

Sauce

  • 4 cups marinara sauce

Instructions

  1. Prepare Eggplant: Salt slices and let sit 20–30 min. Pat completely dry. Brush lightly with olive oil. Roast at 400°F for 20–25 min, flipping once. This prevents sogginess.
  2. Cook Noodles: Cook in well-salted water until just al dente. Drain and lay flat on a towel.
  3. Mix Ricotta Layer: Combine ricotta, egg, 1 cup mozzarella, Parmesan, garlic, herbs, and black pepper.
  4. Assemble in a 9×13 dish: thin layer of marinara, then noodles, roasted eggplant, ricotta mixture, marinara. Repeat layers 2 more times. Finish with sauce and remaining ½ cup mozzarella.
  5. Bake: Cover with foil; bake at 375°F for 35 min. Uncover; bake 10–15 min until bubbling and lightly browned. Rest 15 min before slicing.

Notes

If using no-boil noodles, add an extra ½ cup sauce and ensure noodles are fully covered. This lasagna slices cleanly after resting and reheats well.

Veggie Pad Thai

Serves 2 30 min

Ingredients

  • 5 oz rice noodles
  • Sesame oil
  • ¼ oz unsalted peanuts
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 3 oz silken tofu
  • Reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tsp sweet chili sauce
  • 2 limes
  • 1 shallot
  • 11 oz crunchy veg (asparagus, broccolini, bok choy, baby corn)
  • 3 oz beansprouts
  • 2 large eggs
  • Olive oil
  • Dried chili flakes
  • ½ romaine lettuce
  • ½ bunch fresh basil, mint, and cilantro

Instructions

  1. Cook noodles per package, drain, refresh under cold water, toss with 1 tsp sesame oil.
  2. Lightly toast peanuts in non-stick pan until golden. Bash in pestle and mortar.
  3. Peel garlic, bash to paste with tofu. Add 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tbsp soy, tamarind paste, and chili sauce. Squeeze in half the lime juice.
  4. Finely slice shallot, fry in hot pan. Prep and slice veg, dry-fry 4 min until charred. Add noodles, sauce, beansprouts, and splash of water. Toss 1 min.
  5. Wipe pan. Cook eggs with a little olive oil, sprinkle with chili flakes.
  6. Trim lettuce, place leaves in each bowl. Top with noodles, eggs, herbs, and nuts. Serve with lime wedges.

Notes

To make vegan, remove eggs and serve with extra tofu marinated in soy and lime juice.

  • 12 oz short pasta (farfalle, penne, or casarecce)

  • Red pepper flakes

Vegan Parmesan

Pasta alla Norma

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (16 oz) marinara sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2 medium eggplants (about 2¼ lbs total)

  • 8 oz rigatoni, ziti, or spaghetti

  • ¾ cup (1.5 oz) finely grated ricotta salata and/or Parmesan cheese

  • 1 sweet onion, chopped

  3. Prep eggplant: Use a vegetable peeler to shave off long alternating strips of peel (eggplants will look striped like zebras). Slice into ½-inch thick rounds, discarding end pieces.
