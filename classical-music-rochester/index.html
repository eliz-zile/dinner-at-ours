Organizations

Rochester Philharmonic Orchestra

Over 80 musicians strong, the RPO is Rochester's flagship orchestra. Music Director Andreas Delfs leads Philharmonic, Pops, Sunday Matinee, and special concerts throughout the season.

Primary venue: Kodak Hall at Eastman Theatre · Box Office: (585) 454-2100

Eastman School of Music

Part of the University of Rochester, the Eastman School presents 800+ performances annually — from student recitals to the ticketed Eastman Presents series and Faculty Artist Series.

Kodak Hall, Kilbourn Hall, Hatch Recital Hall · Box Office: (585) 274-3000

Pegasus Early Music

Now in its 21st season, Pegasus presents medieval, renaissance, and baroque music with innovative programming including cross-cultural collaborations. Also runs the free Pegasus Rising young artist series.

Downtown United Presbyterian Church, 121 N. Fitzhugh St.

Society for Chamber Music in Rochester

Founded in 1977, SCMR is Rochester's oldest chamber music society. Artistic Directors Julianna Athayde and Erik Behr present concerts featuring RPO and Eastman musicians.

Beston Hall, Glazer Music Performance Center, Nazareth University

Rochester Oratorio Society

An auditioned symphonic chorus since 1945, the ROS produces 4–6 performances per season and collaborates frequently with the RPO. Also hosts the annual Rochester International Vocal Competition.

Hochstein Performance Hall & Kodak Hall

Rochester Philharmonic Youth Orchestra

A program of the RPO Education Department in association with the Eastman Community Music School, the RPYO motivates young musicians to achieve excellence. Music Director Jherrard Hardeman.

Kodak Hall at Eastman Theatre & Hochstein Performance Hall

The Hochstein School

Founded in 1920, Hochstein is a community music and dance school with an 857-seat Performance Hall. Hosts student concerts, faculty spotlights, and WXXI's "Live from Hochstein" broadcast series.

50 Plymouth Ave N · (585) 454-4596

Rochester Pops Orchestra

Rochester's community pops orchestra performs at Bethel Lutheran Church and other venues, including the "A Festival of Music" series at First Presbyterian Church (free, donations accepted).

Bethel Lutheran Church & First Presbyterian Church

Rochester Music Guild

A community organization that maintains a calendar of musical events across the Rochester area. Contact calendar@rochestermusicguild.org to list your event.

WXXI Classical 91.5

Rochester's classical radio station. Broadcasts RPO concerts, "Live from Hochstein," and the SCMR concert series. Your connection to Rochester's classical music community.

Venues

Kodak Hall at Eastman Theatre

26 Gibbs St. — Rochester's premier concert hall (2,326 seats). Home of the RPO and Eastman's largest performances.

Kilbourn Hall

26 Gibbs St. — An intimate 455-seat recital hall in the Eastman School complex, ideal for chamber music and solo recitals.

Hatch Recital Hall

26 Gibbs St. — The smallest of Eastman's performance spaces, perfect for solo and small ensemble recitals.

Hochstein Performance Hall

50 Plymouth Ave N. — Elegantly renovated 857-seat hall. Hosts student and professional concerts, plus WXXI broadcasts.

Beston Hall, Nazareth University

Glazer Music Performance Center — Home of RPO Sunday Matinees and SCMR chamber concerts. Intimate setting for Mozart and Haydn.

Downtown United Presbyterian Church

121 N. Fitzhugh St. — National Register of Historic Places. Home of Pegasus Early Music. Free parking across the street. ADA accessible.

Christ Church

Home of the Craighead-Saunders Organ and the weekly Eastman Noontime Concert series every Tuesday at 12:10 PM.

Memorial Art Gallery

500 University Ave. — Home of the "Going for Baroque" mini-recital series on the Italian Baroque organ, Sundays at 1:30 & 3:00 PM.
